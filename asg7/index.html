About Benjamin Franklin

Benjamin Franklin: A polymath of the Enlightenment. He was a civic organizer, a diplomat, and one of the drafters and signers of the Declaration of Independence. He championed frugality, self-help, education, and the public good, and epitomized American civic virtue and pragmatism.

Why did he make an outstanding leader?

Franklin was known for his rationality, compromise, and public spirit: he was adept at finding common ground amid disagreements, fostering national consensus through citizen participation and local self-government, and driving long-term prosperity through education and innovation.
